 PREAMBLE  ReachEquilibria (REQ) was founded on June 11, 2024, in Coppell, Texas, as a student-created and student-led nonprofit organization dedicated to mental health advocacy, education, service, awareness, and policy reform. The organization was established following the loss of Shivam Sood, whose passing inspired a commitment to creating meaningful change in the field of mental health. While ReachEquilibria recognizes and preserves its founding story, the organization exists to serve communities everywhere and empower future generations of leaders to create lasting impact. ReachEquilibria believes students possess the ability to lead meaningful initiatives, support their communities, and advocate for a future in which mental health is understood, supported, and prioritized. This Constitution establishes the permanent framework, values, governance structure, and guiding principles of ReachEquilibria.

ARTICLE IV STRATEGIC PILLARS  Pillar I - Advocacy ReachEquilibria promotes student leadership and community engagement in mental health advocacy. The organization encourages students to become advocates for positive change within their schools, communities, and future professions. Pillar II - Policy Reform ReachEquilibria supports long-term efforts that improve mental health systems, education, and access to resources. While remaining nonpartisan, the organization recognizes the importance of meaningful reform that advances mental health awareness, education, and support. Pillar III - Service ReachEquilibria creates direct impact through volunteerism, donations, community service initiatives, and support programs. Service remains a fundamental component of every chapter and organizational initiative. Pillar IV - Education ReachEquilibria promotes understanding and reduces stigma through educational programming, guest speakers, informational campaigns, and community outreach. Pillar V - Awareness ReachEquilibria seeks to increase awareness of mental health challenges, available resources, and opportunities for support through both local and national initiatives.

ARTICLE V CHIEF OFFICERS  Executive leadership exists to protect the mission, build systems, and sustain the organization over time. Section 1. Chief Executive Officer The Chief Executive Officer (CEO) shall serve as the highest executive authority within ReachEquilibria. The CEO shall possess ultimate responsibility for preserving the mission, vision, and long-term sustainability of the organization. The CEO shall have authority to: • Approve chapters • Appoint national leadership • Appoint Chief Officers • Oversee long-term strategy • Guide organizational growth • Approve constitutional amendments • Represent ReachEquilibria publicly • Preserve organizational values and standards The CEO retains final executive authority in all organizational matters. Section 2. Chief Operating Officer The Chief Operating Officer (COO) shall assist in organizational operations and support implementation of strategic initiatives. The COO serves at the discretion of the CEO. Section 3. Additional Chief Officers The CEO may establish additional executive positions as organizational needs evolve. Examples include: • Chief Financial Officer (CFO) • Chief Marketing Officer (CMO) • Chief Advocacy Officer (CAO) • Other executive positions deemed necessary Section 4. Appointment and Removal The CEO retains authority to appoint and remove Chief Officers. Chief Officers serve until resignation, removal, replacement, or transition. Section 5. Executive Continuity ReachEquilibria recognizes the importance of stable executive leadership. Chief Officers shall work to preserve continuity, support organizational growth, and advance the mission of ReachEquilibria.

ARTICLE IX LOCAL CHAPTERS  Section 1. Purpose Local chapters serve as the primary vehicle through which ReachEquilibria fulfills its mission within schools and communities. Each chapter operates under the mission, values, and standards of ReachEquilibria while maintaining flexibility to address local needs. Section 2. Chapter Structure Every active chapter shall maintain the following leadership positions: • President • Vice President • Treasurer • Director of Outreach • Director of Fundraising • Director of Service Additional positions may be created as needed. A chapter must maintain a minimum of six (6) active members. Section 3. Chapter Naming Official chapter names shall follow the format: ReachEquilibria - [School Name] Chapter Example: ReachEquilibria - Coppell High School Chapter Section 4. Chapter Approval All chapters must receive approval from the Chief Executive Officer before receiving official recognition. No chapter may represent itself as an official ReachEquilibria chapter without approval. Section 5. Chapter Leadership Chapter Presidents shall appoint chapter officers. Chapter officers serve until resignation, removal, replacement, or graduation. Advisors and ReachEquilibria National retain authority to remove officers when necessary. Section 6. Chapter Responsibilities All chapters shall: • Uphold the mission of ReachEquilibria • Conduct activities professionally • Submit required reports • Maintain organizational standards • Participate in service, awareness, education, advocacy, and fundraising efforts Section 7. Chapter Autonomy Chapters may determine their own activities, meeting schedules, recruitment strategies, and local initiatives, provided they remain consistent with organizational values and requirements.

ARTICLE XII CHAPTER PRESERVATION POLICY  ReachEquilibria preserves chapter identity even when student leadership changes. Section 1. Purpose ReachEquilibria recognizes that student leadership naturally changes over time. This policy exists to preserve chapter history, accomplishments, and recognition while allowing future students to continue the organization's mission. Section 2. Inactive Chapters Inactive chapters remain part of ReachEquilibria. A chapter shall not be permanently dissolved solely because current leadership graduates, resigns, or becomes inactive. Section 3. Preservation of Legacy Past accomplishments, awards, fundraising achievements, service initiatives, and organizational records shall remain associated with the chapter regardless of temporary inactivity. Section 4. Reactivation Any student may apply to reactivate an inactive chapter. Approval from ReachEquilibria National shall be required before reactivation. Section 5. Chapter Continuity Reactivated chapters shall inherit the history, accomplishments, and records of previous chapter leadership. The chapter's legacy shall remain continuous across generations of student leaders. Section 6. Organizational Commitment ReachEquilibria is committed to building an organization that extends beyond any single group of students. The Chapter Preservation Policy exists to ensure that chapters may continue serving their communities for years to come.

ARTICLE XVII PERMANENT ORGANIZATIONAL COMMITMENTS  ReachEquilibria shall never abandon its commitment to mental health advocacy. PERMANENT COMMITMENT Protected Purpose: ReachEquilibria shall never abandon its commitment to mental health advocacy.  Section 1. Mental Health Advocacy ReachEquilibria shall remain permanently committed to mental health advocacy. This commitment may not be removed from the organization. Section 2. Nonpartisanship ReachEquilibria shall remain a nonpartisan organization. The organization may support mental health advocacy, education, awareness initiatives, and policy discussion but shall not endorse political parties or political candidates. Section 3. Student Leadership ReachEquilibria shall remain committed to developing student leaders and empowering young people to create meaningful change within their communities. Student leadership shall remain a defining characteristic of the organization. Section 4. Community Impact ReachEquilibria shall strive to create measurable and sustainable impact through service, education, awareness, advocacy, and future policy reform efforts. Section 5. Organizational Growth The organization shall pursue growth that is sustainable, mission-aligned, and capable of creating long-term impact. Section 6. Evolution The following may evolve as organizational needs change: • Programs • Strategic initiatives • Organizational structure • Leadership positions • Branding • Mission statement language • Tagline language Such changes must remain consistent with the purpose, values, and commitments established by this Constitution. Section 7. Protected Purpose ReachEquilibria shall never abandon its commitment to mental health advocacy. Any future evolution of the organization must remain connected to advancing mental health awareness, education, support, service, advocacy, or reform.

ARTICLE XIX CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS  Section 1. Purpose This Constitution exists to provide a stable framework for the governance, operation, and future development of ReachEquilibria. At the same time, the organization recognizes the importance of adaptation and continuous improvement. This Article establishes the procedures through which the Constitution may be amended. Section 2. Amendment Authority Constitutional amendments may be proposed by: • The Chief Executive Officer • Active Chief Officers • The Board of Directors Proposed amendments should be submitted in writing and clearly identify the section or sections being modified. Section 3. Review Process Prior to approval, proposed amendments may be reviewed by: • Chief Officers • The Board of Directors • The National Advisory Board • Other individuals designated by the Chief Executive Officer The review process exists to ensure amendments remain consistent with the mission and long-term vision of ReachEquilibria. Section 4. Approval Constitutional amendments shall require approval from the Chief Executive Officer. Upon approval, amendments shall become official components of this Constitution. Section 5. Protected Principles The following principles shall remain protected and may not be removed without extraordinary justification: • Commitment to mental health advocacy • Nonpartisan organizational status • Preservation of the Founder's Legacy • Student-led organizational philosophy • The mission-centered purpose of ReachEquilibria Section 6. Effective Date Approved amendments shall become effective immediately unless otherwise specified within the amendment itself. Section 7. Recordkeeping ReachEquilibria shall maintain a record of all constitutional amendments. The Document Control Page shall be updated to reflect future revisions and version history.

CONCLUSION  ReachEquilibria was founded on the belief that students possess the ability to create meaningful and lasting change.
