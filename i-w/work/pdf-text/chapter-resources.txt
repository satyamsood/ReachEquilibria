How to Use This Packet
This packet is the practical operating toolkit for ReachEquilibria chapters. The Constitution explains 
what ReachEquilibria is. This packet explains how to start and run it at the chapter level.
Presidents should use this packet during chapter launch, monthly planning, officer transitions, 
sponsor outreach, and event preparation. Advisors should use it to understand expectations and 
support student leadership without taking control of the chapter.
CHAPTER PHILOSOPHY
National alignment. Local freedom. Chapters are expected to uphold the mission, submit reports, and 
represent the brand professionally while adapting events and recruitment to their own community.
Resource Best Used For
Chapter Launch Checklist Starting a new chapter from zero
First 30 Days Plan Setting up leadership, advisor support, and first 
meeting
First 90 Days Plan Completing first event, report, and recruitment cycle
Officer Role Cards Clarifying responsibilities

Programming Playbooks
Donation Drive
Donation drives are one of the easiest ways for new chapters to create direct service impact. 
Chapters may collect clothing, hygiene products, school supplies, care items, or other community 
needs.
Step Action
1 Pick a specific need
2 Find a recipient organization
3 Promote collection dates
4 Track items collected
5 Deliver donations and take photos
6 Report individuals reached and items donated
Guest Speaker Event
Guest speaker events support the education pillar by inviting therapists, counselors, advocates, 
nonprofit leaders, or community members to educate students and families.
Step Action
1 Choose topic
2 Invite speaker
3 Reserve space
4 Promote event
5 Host Q&A
6 Submit photos and attendance estimate
Advocacy Campaign
A chapter advocacy campaign may include daily social media videos, informational posts, resource 
sharing, school announcements, or awareness tables.
Campaign Element Example
Length One week or one month
Platform Instagram, TikTok, school announcements
Content Facts, resources, leader messages, event reminders
Verification Screenshots and engagement estimates

Fundraising and Sponsorship Guide
Chapters keep the money they raise. National does not automatically fund chapter events. Because 
of this, every chapter should learn basic fundraising and sponsorship outreach.
Common Fundraising Models
Model Description
Tournament or Competition Basketball, chess, pickleball, or school-based event
Restaurant Night Local restaurant donates percentage of sales
Business Sponsorship Business contributes money or goods for recognition
Donation Drive Collect goods instead of money
Community Fundraiser Bake sale, car wash, school fair, wellness event
Sponsor Tier Template
Tier Suggested Contribution Recognition
Bronze $100-$249 Social media thank-you
Silver $250-$499 Logo on event materials
Gold $500-$999 Logo, social post, event recognition
Platinum $1,000+ Premium recognition and speaking 
opportunity if appropriate
IMPORTANT
Sponsorship tiers may depend on the event. Chapters should consult National for major sponsorship 
packages or unusual sponsor requests.

Branding and Social Media Guidelines
Chapters have freedom to create social media accounts and promotional materials, but they must 
represent ReachEquilibria professionally and notify National of active accounts.
Brand Element Standard
Name Format ReachEquilibria — [School Name] Chapter
Logo Use official REQ logo; do not distort or recolor without 
approval
Colors Black, white, olive green, mental health awareness 
green
Tone Professional, hopeful, supportive, student-led
Posts Avoid partisan, controversial, unsafe, or misleading 
content
Account Info President should notify National of account handles
